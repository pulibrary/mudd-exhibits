Stevenson’s endorsement of Kennedy
Though twice defeated in the general election after winning his party’s nomination in the 1950s, Stevenson was still considered by some, including himself, to be a possible candidate for a third run for the presidency in 1960. However, John F. Kennedy’s strong primary campaign led to his claiming the nomination on the first ballot, locking out the competition. Stevenson, who had assumed the mantle of Democratic party leadership throughout the 1950s, gracefully endorsed Kennedy at the Democratic National Convention. In that speech, he mocked Eisenhower’s caretaker presidency, made self-effacing remarks about his own political career, and extolled John Kennedy’s virtues. In this clip, Stevenson notes the proud history of the Democratic Party as well as the hypocrisy of the 1960 Republican nominee, Richard Nixon.

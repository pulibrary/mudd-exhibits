"I’m Adlai Stevenson"
In 1956, television was still a relative newcomer to presidential politics. Both Stevenson and his opponent, Dwight Eisenhower, made numerous short commercials like this one in which they looked directly into the camera and spoke about the issues. In these four spots, Stevenson discusses rural electrification, foreign policy, labor unions, and civil rights. While these have a tag line for the Pennsylvania primary, most likely they were used in various primary elections, as well as in the run-up to the general election. Because Stevenson was the Democratic frontrunner in 1956, he focused his attention on his Republican opponents.

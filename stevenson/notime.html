"This is not the time for superficial solutions..."
Despite his protestations, the delegates drafted Stevenson in 1952, and he accepted the nomination at the Democratic National Convention in Chicago with a speech that according to contemporaries, "electrified the nation." The speech, a snippet of which is presented here, was filled with Biblical allegory and carefully crafted phrases that emphasized his command of the English language. Stevenson's distinctive speaking style quickly earned him the reputation of an intellectual and endeared him to many Americans, while simultaneously alienating him from others.

"Sheerest Coincidence"
With the wit and self-effacement that were his trademarks, Stevenson explains how he became involved in politics, noting his father and grandfather’s political activities. His paternal grandfather, Adlai E. Stevenson, served as Grover Cleveland's Vice President during his second term (1893-1897), was nominated for the same office with William Jennings Bryan in 1900, and ran unsuccessfully for Illinois governor in 1908. Lewis G. Stevenson, his father, served as Illinois Secretary of State. In this clip from a 1960 interview program produced by Stanford University students, Stevenson explains how he came to run for governor of Illinois in 1948.

Vote for Stevenson!
In this, one of America’s first television political commercials, a series of sketches details the American farm situation and its importance to the American economy. Harking back to 1931, it recalls the Depression years and the suffering of farmers. The year was chosen not only because "1931" rhymed with Stevenson, but also because in the spring of that year, a slightly recovering American economy was beaten down when the European banking system collapsed, dashing hopes that the then 18-month old depression might soon be over. For many Americans long-accustomed to Democrats in the White House, the worry that Republican control could bring about another Depression was real.
